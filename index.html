Welcome to Pretoria Leinster Lodge #743 I.C.

Welcome to our lodge, a place of brotherhood, heritage, and community service. At Pretoria Leinster, we uphold the proud traditions of Irish Freemasonry, fostering a spirit of kinship and philanthropy.

About Freemasonry

Freemasonry is a world-renowned fraternal organization known for its commitment to helping individuals lead ethical lives of service and integrity. Our lodge embodies these principles, creating a supportive and enriching environment for our members.

Our Activities

Our lodge is actively involved in various charitable projects and fellowship events. These activities are at the heart of our mission to make a positive impact in our community and provide a space for personal growth and camaraderie among our members.

Join Our Brotherhood

If you are drawn to the values of Freemasonry and wish to be part of our brotherhood, we invite you to learn more about joining Pretoria Leinster Lodge. Please feel free to reach out to us for more information on becoming a member.

Origins1

In 1772, Cape Town heralded the inception of its inaugural Masonic lodge, Lodge De Goede Hoop. This establishment marked the beginning of a flourishing Masonic influence. By 1812, the English Grand Lodge had established the British Lodge, and by 1860, the Scottish made their presence known. The 1890s saw the introduction of Irish Freemasonry to South Africa. By 1898, under the leadership of W.Bro. Dr. E.H. Croghan from Abercom Lodge No.159, the initiative for a Provincial Grand Lodge began. Following extensive consultations with Deputy Grand Secretary V.W.Bro. H.E. Flavelle, a unanimous decision was reached, leading to a formal petition to the Grand Master.

Affiliated with Abercom Lodge since 1897, R.W. Bro. Kendal Franks played an instrumental role in this foundational period. An esteemed alumnus of the University of Dublin, Franks was renowned as a distinguished surgeon. His relocation to South Africa, primarily for his wife's health, turned out to be a boon for the region. Recognized for his valor during the tumultuous Anglo-Boer War, his exemplary efforts earned him a knighthood from King Edward VII.

St. Patrick's Lodge No. 199 in Cape Town exhibited reservations about the swift establishment of an Irish Provincial Grand Lodge. Concerns encompassed logistical challenges due to the vast territory and governance qualms. Nevertheless, with unwavering determination, Abercorn Lodge forwarded their petition to Dublin by October 1898.

Abercom Lodge's Minutes showed restraint until July 1899. However, the Anglo-Boer War introduced unforeseen challenges, including the untimely passing of W.Bro. E.H. Croghan. Amid these challenges, R.W. Bro. Kendal Franks rose to prominence. By 1904, the Grand Lodge sanctioned the 1898 petition, and R.W.Bro Kendal Franks became the first Provincial Grand Master of the Provincial Grand Lodge (I.C). The first Quarterly Communication of the Provincial Grand Lodge of South Africa (I.C) was held 13 February, 1905, at the Plein Street Masonic temple in Johannesburg.

Even before the official establishment of the Provincial Grand Lodge of Ireland, several lodges were actively affiliated with the Grand Lodge of Ireland:

  • Cape Province:
    • St. Patrick's Lodge No. 199 - 1898
    • Killarney Lodge No.360 - 1903
    • Excelsior Lodge No.368 - 1903
  • Natal:
    • Hibernia Lodge No.365 - 1903
  • Northern Region:
    • Abercor Lodge No. 159 (Oldest Irish Lodge in Africa) - 1895
    • Edward H. Croghan Lodge No.247 - 1898
    • Kendal Franks Lodge No.265 - 1899
    • Shamrock Lodge No.338 - 1903
    • Connaught Lodge No.362 - 1903
    • Erin Lodge No.364 - 1903
    • Erin-go-Bragh Lodge No.370 - 1903

Masonic organization in the region saw significant milestones with the establishment of the Provincial Grand Lodge (I.C) in the Southern Cape in 1925 and in Natal in 1932.

Founding - 1950

Establishment of Pretoria Leinster Lodge No 743 - 19501

R.W Bro E.W. Snell, after receiving his patent from M. W. the Grand Master, the Earl of Donoughmore, was solemnly invested on 20th October 1945. This ceremony was graced by the heads of other Constitutions and his brother, V. W. Bro P. Snell, a member of the Southern Cape Provincial Grand Lodge. The officer presiding over the installation was V. W. Bro. Victor Hall.

The primary officers invested by the newly installed R. W. Provincial Grand Master were:

  • Provincial Senior Grand Warden W. Bro J. D. Rossouw
  • Provincial Junior Grand Warden W. Bro R. J. Davis
  • Provincial Grand Treasurer V. W. Bro A.J. Kenward
  • Provincial Grand Secretary V. W. Bro J.R. Wolfe

During R. W. Bro. Snell's tenure, five new lodges were added to the Roll of the Grand Lodge of Ireland:

  • Primrose Lodge No. 710 in 1946 at Germiston - W. Bro A.W. Hirst
  • Shannon Lodge No. 729 in 1948 at Vanderbijl Park- W. Bro. J.A. Lock
  • Pretoria Leinster Lodge No. 743 in 1950 at Pretoria - W. Bro. A.J. Cornelius
  • Lurgan Lodge No. 767 in 1953 at Johannesburg - V. W. Bro. H.A. Murray
  • Tzaneen Lodge No. 773 in 1955 at Duiwelskloof - W. Bro. Col. J.P. Coetzee

The P.G.M officiated at all Consecrations.

Origins: Late 19th Century to Early 20th Century1

The first recorded desire to establish a Provincial Grand Lodge in South Africa traces back to a minute from Abercom Lodge No.159 on 29th August 1898. Driven by the presence of three active lodges under the Irish Constitution, W.Bro. Dr. E.H. Croghan initiated the discourse. Following detailed exchanges with Deputy Grand Secretary V.W.Bro. H.E. Flavelle, the decision was unanimous: a petition would be sent to the Grand Master for a Charter and to appoint W.Bro. Dr. Edward Henry Croghan as Provincial Grand Master.

Significant Contributions of R.W. Bro. Kendal Franks

R.W. Bro. Kendal Franks, associated with Abercom Lodge since August 1897, emerged as a pivotal figure during these foundational moments. Born in February 1851, Franks was related to notable families, excelling academically at the University of Dublin and making a name for himself as a prominent surgeon. His relocation to South Africa, initially due to his wife's health, brought his expertise to the region. Amidst the challenges of the Anglo-Boer War, wherein he served with distinction as a Consulting Surgeon, he stood out for his courage and service. Despite some criticisms during the war, his reputation was largely untarnished, leading to his knighthood by King Edward VII.

St. Patrick's Lodge Declines

While Abercorn Lodge spearheaded the initiative, St. Patrick's Lodge No. 199 in Cape Town had reservations. Their hesitance centered on the perceived premature need for a Provincial Grand Lodge, logistical challenges owing to the vast geographical distances, and governance concerns about being directed from the South African Republic. This did not deter Abercorn Lodge, which promptly relayed their petition and related correspondence to Dublin by 31st October 1898.

Grand Lodge Deliberations

The Grand Lodge deliberated extensively. The Abercom Lodge Minutes were notably silent on this matter until July 1899. However, the Anglo-Boer War brought unforeseen challenges, including the sad demise of W.Bro. E.H. Croghan. It was in these trying times that R.W. Bro. Kendal Franks stepped up as the successor. By 3rd March 1904, the Grand Lodge had given a positive nod to the 1898 petition, and the Duke of Abercorn appointed R.W.Bro Kendal Franks as the first Provincial Grand Master of South Africa. This was a title made even more prestigious by his subsequent knighthood.

Establishment of Deputy Grand Master

By May 1904, the Masonic leadership continued to evolve. Under the guidance of W.Bro. Dr. W.M. Russell, the Provincial Grand Master, W.Bro. Major T.M.M. Fuge was inaugurated as the Provincial Deputy Grand Master.

Masonic Lodges Pre-Provincial Grand Lodge Era

Even before the Provincial Grand Lodge came into official existence, a range of lodges were actively operating under the Grand Lodge of Ireland:

  • Cape Province:
    • St. Patrick's Lodge No. 199 - 1898
    • Killarney Lodge No.360 - 1903
    • Excelsior Lodge No.368 - 1903
  • Natal:
    • Hibernia Lodge No.365 - 1903
  • In the North:
    • Abercor Lodge No. 159 (Oldest Irish Lodge in Africa) - 1895
    • Edward H. Croghan Lodge No.247 - 1898
    • Kendal Franks Lodge No.265 - 1899
    • Shamrock Lodge No.338 - 1903
    • Connaught Lodge No.362 - 1903
    • Erin Lodge No.364 - 1903
    • Erin-go-Bragh Lodge No.370 - 1903

The journey towards Masonic organization in the region saw notable landmarks in 1925 and 1932 with the foundation of the Provincial Grand Lodge in the Southern Cape and Natal, respectively.

Founding - 1950

Establishment of Pretoria Leinster Lodge No 743 - 19501

R.W Bro E.W. Snell, after receiving his patent from M. W. the Grand Master, the Earl of Donoughmore, was solemnly invested on 20th October 1945. This ceremony was graced by the heads of other Constitutions and his brother, V. W. Bro P. Snell, a member of the Southern Cape Provincial Grand Lodge. The officer presiding over the installation was V. W. Bro. Victor Hall.

The primary officers invested by the newly installed R. W. Provincial Grand Master were:

  • Provincial Senior Grand Warden W. Bro J. D. Rossouw
  • Provincial Junior Grand Warden W. Bro R. J. Davis
  • Provincial Grand Treasurer V. W. Bro A.J. Kenward
  • Provincial Grand Secretary V. W. Bro J.R. Wolfe

During R. W. Bro. Snell's tenure, five new lodges were added to the Roll of the Grand Lodge of Ireland:

  • Primrose Lodge No. 710 in 1946 at Germiston - W. Bro A.W. Hirst
  • Shannon Lodge No. 729 in 1948 at Vanderbijl Park- W. Bro. J.A. Lock
  • Pretoria Leinster Lodge No. 743 in 1950 at Pretoria - W. Bro. A.J. Cornelius
  • Lurgan Lodge No. 767 in 1953 at Johannesburg - V. W. Bro. H.A. Murray
  • Tzaneen Lodge No. 773 in 1955 at Duiwelskloof - W. Bro. Col. J.P. Coetzee

The P.G.M officiated at all Consecrations.
